EcoSpool

Developing a modified small-scale, industrial-quality plastic extrusion system prototype tailored to enable polypropylene waste recycling and filament making at NUS.

CDE4301 · AY2025/26 Sem 2 · iDP Final Year Project

There is a plastic recycling issue at the National University of Singapore (NUS), where the institution produces large volumes of polypropylene (PP) waste from laboratory consumables and pipette tip boxes. Nearly all of this material is currently sent for incineration, despite being technically recyclable.

The NUS Sustainability Strategy Unit tasked us to find ways to recycle and increase the value of such PP waste. Our solution is developing a modified small-scale, industrial-quality plastic extrusion system prototype specifically tailored to enable PP waste recycling and filament making. Our report covers the methodological approach in designing the system, isolating variables that affect filament quality, and how our system maintains a small footprint and is easily replicable at an institutional level to re-value PP waste in the plastic recycling ecosystem.
