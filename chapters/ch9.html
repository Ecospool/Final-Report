09

Limitations & Future Work

Identified constraints and the roadmap for EcoSpool v2 and beyond.

9.1 Achieving Spool-Level Diameter Uniformity

The primary remaining challenge is sustaining diameter uniformity across a full 500 g spool. Longer extrusion runs showed drift correlated with hopper level, suggesting a level-controlled feed mechanism as the priority next step.

9.2 Feedstock Contamination

Post-consumer PP flakes require pre-sorting and washing to remove paper, glue, and reagent residues. An inline contamination detection step (NIR or density sorting) would significantly improve output consistency.

9.3 Scaling Throughput

The current system produces approximately 50–80 g/hr. Increasing L:D ratio (to 24:1) and screw speed range would improve throughput to the 200 g/hr target for meaningful lab-scale recycling volume.

9.4 Advanced Screw and Mixing Designs

Barrier screws and Maddock mixing elements would improve melt homogeneity for high-contamination feedstocks. Computational screw design optimisation using PP rheological data (NETZSCH) is recommended for v2.
