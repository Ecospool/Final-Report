Cooling Systems

Concept selection, wind flow simulation, prototyping, and the Omni-Flow annular cooling design.

6.1 Concept Selection

6.1.2 Water vs Air Cooling

Water cooling offers higher heat extraction rates but introduces slip, surface contamination risk, and drying requirements. Air cooling was selected for simplicity and compatibility with the lab setting.

6.1.3 Fan Choice and Configuration

Brushless 40 mm × 40 mm DC fans (12 V) were selected for low vibration and controllable speed via PWM. Multiple fans arranged radially provide symmetric airflow.

6.2 Simulation of Wind Flow (Multi-axis Cooling, Parallel Airstream)

6.2.1 Evenness of Cooling

CFD-informed airflow modelling showed that a single fan produces a cooling asymmetry of up to 18 °C across the filament cross-section, driving ovality. Four-fan symmetric arrangement reduced asymmetry to under 3 °C.

6.2.2 Effectiveness of Cooling

Filament surface temperature drops from ~200 °C at the die exit to below PP's crystallisation onset (~130 °C) within 120 mm of the die with fan speed setting 1 — within the available cooling duct length.

6.2.3 Thermal Analysis

Heat balance confirms forced convection with four 40 mm fans provides sufficient cooling capacity to solidify the filament before the take-up spool under all operating screw speeds (2–6.7 rpm).

6.3 Preliminary Prototyping

Initial prototypes tested two-, four-, and six-fan configurations. Four-fan configuration achieved the best balance of symmetry and compactness. Six-fan introduced turbulent interactions that degraded uniformity.

6.4 Omni-Flow

The Omni-Flow is a 3D-printed annular duct surrounding the filament at 40 mm from the die exit. Internal baffles channel airflow from four radial fans into a co-axial laminar stream, ensuring circumferential symmetry and eliminating one-sided quenching. Best result: σ = 0.183 mm with fan speed 1 + spooling.

6.5 Results & Conclusion

The Omni-Flow assembly reduced filament ovality by 62% compared to single-fan lateral cooling. It was adopted as the final cooling configuration for the integrated prototype.
