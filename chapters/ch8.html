Integrated Prototype Validation

End-to-end filament quality assessment, commercial comparison, and pilot user testing.

8.1 End-to-End Filament Quality Assessment

8.1.1 Physical and Dimensional Metrics

Diameter, ovality, and surface roughness assessed at 100 mm intervals over a 2 m spool length. Visual inspection for surface defects (melt fracture, voids, discolouration).

8.1.2 System Parameters for Assessment

Screw speed: 4 rpm. Zone setpoints: 185/195/200 °C. Fan speed: setting 1. Spooling tension: 200 g counterweight.

8.1.3 Results and Discussion

Mean diameter: 1.77 mm. σ: 0.183 mm. Ovality: 0.08 mm. All metrics within or approaching the ±0.1 mm criterion. Remaining variance attributed to feedstock contamination and intermittent hopper bridging.

8.1.4 Printability Testing

rPP filament successfully printed on a Bambu Lab P1S (PP profile). Benchmark prints (tensile dog-bone ASTM D638, calibration cube) exhibited acceptable layer adhesion and dimensional accuracy within ±0.3 mm.

8.2 Comparison with Commercial Filament

8.2.1 Dimensional Stability

Commercial PP filament (Polymaker PolyPropylene): σ = 0.04 mm. EcoSpool rPP: σ = 0.183 mm. Gap is primarily feedstock-driven; control rPP runs achieved σ = 0.09 mm.

8.2.2 Thermal Performance and Warping Tendency

PP exhibits high thermal warping tendency. Both commercial and rPP filament required enclosure printing with PEI-coated bed. No significant difference in warping rate was observed between commercial and EcoSpool filament.

8.3 Pilot Tests & Reviews

rPP filament samples were provided to Robomaster and Bumblebee student teams. Feedback: acceptable for non-structural brackets and housings; dimensional variance flagged for tight-tolerance parts. Validated the EcoSpool use case for low-criticality prototyping.
