Design Methodology

Systematic approach from problem decomposition through concept generation to a validated design statement.

3.1 Design Approach

Two root issues (§2.4) drove four solution streams evaluated via a morphological chart spanning 20 functional subsystems.

3.1.1 Solution 1-A: Even Material Compression & Mixing

Compression screws eliminate feed-zone air voids and increase bulk density before melting, stabilising throughput rate.

3.1.2 Solution 1-B: Uniform Heating Gradient

Multi-zone barrel heating with calibrated setpoints ensures complete and consistent plastication along the screw length.

3.1.3 Solution 2-A: Uniform Cooling Coverage (Circumferential)

Radially symmetric airflow prevents one-sided quenching that causes ovality in the solidifying filament.

3.1.4 Solution 2-B: Even Cross-Sectional Cooling Gradient

Parallel airstreams from multiple axes ensure the core and skin of the filament cool at compatible rates.

3.2 Design Statement

"To modify the Felfil single-screw extruder with a compression screw, multi-zone barrel heating, and an omni-directional cooling assembly to produce 1.75 ± 0.1 mm filament from post-consumer PP flake feedstock at steady-state throughput."

3.3 System Overview

The integrated EcoSpool system comprises four primary subsystems: (1) feed hopper with agitator, (2) modified compression-screw barrel with three-zone heating, (3) Omni-Flow annular cooling duct, and (4) dual-axis dial-gauge quality monitoring with take-up spool.

3.5 Experimentation Methodology

3.5.1 Data Measurement

Dual-axis digital dial gauges positioned 200 mm downstream of the die. Readings logged via RS-232 at 1 Hz, supplemented by video-based OCR for redundancy.

3.5.2 Data Processing

Diameter readings processed in Python/Jupyter. Metrics: rolling mean (window = 60 s), σ, and ovality. Steady-state defined as the interval after the first 10 minutes of extrusion.
